Sensitivity Analysis

This section reruns the simulation under small changes to key inputs and compares the resulting KPIs. It helps show which assumptions most influence completion rate, patient touchpoints, and total time.
Why sensitivity matters: Small changes in scheduling success, patient demand, or appointment availability can significantly affect patient access and provider workload.
How to read this
  • Higher completion rate is generally better.
  • Lower touchpoints means less patient effort.
  • Lower total time means faster access and completion.
  • If a small parameter change moves KPIs a lot, the system is sensitive to that assumption.
